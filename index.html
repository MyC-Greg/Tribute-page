A tribute to Albert Camus: writer of Freedom

The only way to deal with an unfree world is to become so absolutely free that your very existence is an act of rebellion.

Short biography

  • Albert Camus was a French-Algerian journalist, playwright, novelist, philosophical essayist, and Nobel laureate.
  • He is mostly know for his literary work: his three most famous novels:
    • The Stranger,
    • The Plague,
    • and The Fall
    and two book-length philosophical essays:
    • The Myth of Sisyphus,
    • and The Rebel,
    that granted him international reputation and readership.
  • He developed a phylosophy based on two main concepts:
    • the Absurd
    • and The Revolt that refers to both a path of resolved action and a state of mind.
  • His political views established him as an outspoken champion of individual freedom and as an impassioned critic of tyranny and terrorism, whether practiced by the Left or by the Right.
  • He himself took action in the French Resistance against Nazism during the second World War.
    He concluded his Nobel acceptance speech with this stirring reminder that can extend to every citizen: “The nobility of our craft,” he declared, “will always be rooted in two commitments, both difficult to maintain: the refusal to lie about what one knows and the resistance to oppression.”
